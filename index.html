What the App Does

Real ER triage logic, simplified for parents.

🌡️

14 Symptom Categories

Fever, breathing problems, rashes, vomiting, head injuries, seizures, allergic reactions, and more. Every common pediatric symptom covered.

👶

Age-Specific Guidance

A fever in a newborn is very different from a fever in a 5-year-old. The app adjusts its recommendations based on your child's exact age group.

🚦

Color-Coded Results

Clear green/yellow/orange/red recommendations. No medical jargon. You'll know exactly what to do in seconds.

💊

OTC Dosing Cards

Weight-based dosing for Tylenol, Motrin, and Benadryl. No more Googling doses at 3 AM.

⏱️

Medication Timer

Set reminders so you never double-dose or miss a dose. Tracks Tylenol and Motrin alternating schedules.

📚

Evidence-Based References

Every recommendation is backed by AAP guidelines, ACEP clinical policies, and peer-reviewed medical literature. Tap to see the sources.

Simple, Clear Answers

Every assessment ends with one of four color-coded recommendations.

🏠
Monitor at Home
Your child is likely fine. Here's what to watch for.
📞
Call Your Doctor
Not an emergency, but worth a call in the next few hours.
🏥
Urgent Care
Needs attention today. Head to urgent care or your pediatrician.
🚨
Go to the ER
Don't wait. Go now or call 911.

Built by an ER Doctor Who Gets It

Dr. Oliver Morris is a board-certified emergency medicine physician who sees hundreds of worried parents every month. He also has four kids of his own.

He built Should I Worry? because most parents who come to his ER at 2 AM don't actually need the ER. They need someone to tell them it's going to be OK, or that yes, this one needs attention.

This app gives you that guidance instantly, based on the same triage protocols ER doctors use.
